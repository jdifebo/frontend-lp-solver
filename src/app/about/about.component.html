Optimizing Nutrients using Linear Programming

What is linear programming?

Linear programming is an optimization method that can be applied to systems of linear constraints. For example, if you have the following constraints: 

    21x + 3y + 8z > 56
    7y + 76z > 130

You could then optimize for the function: 

    Minimize: 119*x + 34*y + 362*z

Great, why is that useful?

A variety of problems can be solved by realizing that they can be converted into a bunch of constraint functions. For example, consider the following hypothetical scenario. You look in your kitchen and you realize that the only food that you have is chicken, broccoli, and brown rice. Due to your new diet, you know that you need at least 56 grams of protein and 130 grams of carbohydrates every day. But you also want to lose weight, so you are attempting to minimize the number of calories that you eat every day. After thinking back to your algorithms coursework, you realize that this problem is perfect for linear programming! You look up the nutrition information for your 3 foods and enter the values in a table as follows:
Chicken Broccoli Brown Rice Daily Goal
Protein (g) 21 3 8 ≥56
Carbs (g) 0 7 76 ≥130
Calories (kcal) 119 34 362 Minimize
